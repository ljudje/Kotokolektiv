Re-Videk / Author: Anže Mrak / Mentor: Prof. Nataša Peršuh / 2021 Link to the project’s website: www.anzemrak.com/re-videk
Kolekcija »Re-Videk« je nastajala v postkoronskem obdobju pod okriljem naslova Re-De-Fi-Ni, kjer so oblikovalci na različne načine iskali svoje avtorske odgovore na izzive nove realnosti, se preizpraševali o redefiniciji življenja po pandemiji v najširšem pomenu besede, zlasti o redefiniciji na področju mode in oblikovanja oblačil in tekstilij.
The “Re-Videk” collection was created in the post-pandemic period, under the auspices of the title the Re-De-Fi-Ni, where designers were in various ways looking for unique answers to the challenges of the new reality. They were questioning the redefinition of life after the pandemic in the broadest sense, especially about redefinition in fashion and textile design.
Vsebinsko ozadje projekta Re-Videk je bil odziv na čas in trenutne okoliščine. Sprašuje se, kje je meja med realnim in virtualnim, kje so novi limiti estetike v modnem izrazu, kateri mediji so danes bolj relevantni. Kolekcija Re-Videk se inspirira v ikonični slovenski pravljici »Kdo je napravil Vidku srajčico?«, v kateri Videk, najmlajši izmed otrok v revni družini, vedno nosi samo ponošena in strgana oblačila. Ker pa je Videk dobrega srca in ima poseben čut za živali, mu živali pomagajo do nove srajčice.
The content background of the “Re-Videk” project was a response to the current circumstances. The project raised questions about the boundary between the real and the virtual, the new limits of aesthetics in fashion expression and the more relevant media today. The “Re-Videk” collection is inspired by the iconic Slovenian fairy tale Who Made Videk’s Shirt?, in which Videk, the youngest child in a poor family, always wears only worn and torn clothes. However, because Videk has a kind heart and has a special relationship with animals, the animals help him get a new shirt.
Prva izdaja te pravljice (1979), ki je bila kasneje še večkrat ponatisnjena, je ilustrirana v specifičnem prepoznavnem stilu (ilustratorka Roža Piščanec), katerega podobe so se usidrale v podzavest celih generacij otrok, vključno z avtorjem, ki posledično v svoji kolekciji izpostavlja minimalistično estetiko, otroško preprostost in belino sveže, nove srajčice.
The first edition of this fairy tale (1979), later reprinted several times, is illustrated in a specific and recognisable style (illustrator Roža Piščanec). These images have been anchored in the subconscious of entire generations of children, including the author’s subconscious, who consequently, in his collection, emphasises minimalistic aesthetics, childlike simplicity and the whiteness of a fresh, new shirt.

O projektu / about the project
Izbruh pandemije COVID-19 v začetku leta 2020 je močno vplival na modno in tekstilno industrijo, ki se je tako rekoč čez noč ustavila in spremenila življenja ljudi, ki na različnih segmentih delujejo v njeni verigi. Prav tako je močno vplivala na življenje prihodnje generacije oblikovalcev tekstilij in oblačil. Teoretični pouk in praktični študijski proces sta se preselila v spletne učilnice, kar je zahtevalo skoraj nemogoče prilagoditve. Izzivi, s katerimi smo se soočili tako pedagoški delavci kot študenti, so bili nepredstavljivi. Panogi, katere poglavitni značilnosti sta taktilnost in materialnost, je bil onemogočen otip, pogled od blizu in v prostoru, na telesu.
‍
Začetek pandemije marca 2020 je pretresel tudi naše javne predstavitve, zato smo se v duhu časa prilagodili novim razmeram in ustvarili spletno platformo kotofolio.si. Sodobno oblikovana spletna stran širši javnosti v slovenskem jeziku predstavlja najbolj relevantne študentske projekte od študijskega leta 2019/20 naprej, obenem pa omogoča vpogled v izhodiščne koncepte, raziskovanja, razvoj idej in končne produkte skozi fotografije in gibljive slike.

Katedra za oblikovanje tekstilij in oblačil, OTGO,

The outbreak of the COVID-19 pandemic at the beginning of 2020 had a profound impact on the fashion and textile industry, which came to a halt overnight and changed the lives of individuals working in various segments of its supply chain. It also significantly affected the lives of the future generation of textile and clothing designers. The theoretical and practical lessons moved to online classrooms, which demanded almost impossible adjustments. The challenges faced by educators and students were unimaginable. For an industry whose primary characteristics are tactility and materiality, the sense of touch, close-up views and spatial perception were made impossible.
‍
The onset of the pandemic in March 2020 also shook up our public presentations. In the spirit of the times, we adapted to the new circumstances and created the online platform kotofolio.si. The website, in the Slovenian language, presents the most relevant student projects from the academic year 2019/20 onwards to the broader public. Additionally, it provides insight into the initial concepts, research, and development of ideas and final products through photographs and moving images.
